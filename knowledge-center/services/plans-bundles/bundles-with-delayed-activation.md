# Bundles with delayed activation

**Bundles with delayed activation**

In some of our offers, including Ubigi and Service Provider Connect, we have introduced the “**delayed activation”**.  
  
Unlike classic bundles, bundles with delayed activation will not activate automatically at subscription.  
Instead, they will activate only when data traffic is made in a destination included in the bundle’s coverage.  
  
This enables fleet administrators to add plans ahead of time, ensuring no validity days are lost.  
  
For example, a **Japan Plan** added to a French traveler’s SIM will stay “waiting for 1st use in country” until the traveler connects to a Japanese network.  
  
Plans waiting for activation will be clearly displayed as “waiting” in our interface.  
  
**Important note:**  
If a plan isn’t activated within 6 months of subscription, it will be automatically terminated without refund.  
  
![image](/knowledge-center/assets/image-1.png)

**Stacking bundles with delayed activation**

Multiple bundles with the same destination(s) included in their coverage can be stacked:  
  
– **Case 1:** For a traveler heading to a single destination for 36 days, you can add a **30-day plan** followed by a **7-day plan**. The second plan activates only after the first expires.  
  
– **Case 2:** For a traveler visiting **the USA for 10 days** and **Canada for 4 days** you can add a **USA 7-day plan** plus a **Canada+USA 7-day plan**, with the Canada+USA plan activating only if the USA plan is exhausted or when the traveler reaches Canada.  
  
Note: if an “unlimited” plan is currently being throttled, other plans covering the same location will take priority to enable the end-user to benefit from the full speed.
